where hardware meets software

2024.12 · 6 min

Building Amiwo — a personal UV and air quality sensor — has been a lesson in the strange space where atoms meet bits.

Software people often underestimate hardware. Hardware people often underestimate software. Both are wrong. The convergence zone is where interesting things happen.

atoms are slow, bits are fast

In software, iteration is free. Deploy, measure, fix, deploy again. The cycle can be hours or minutes.

In hardware, iteration costs. Each PCB revision takes weeks. Each enclosure redesign needs new molds. You learn to think more carefully before committing.

This constraint is frustrating but valuable. It forces design — actual upfront thinking about what you're building and why.

the BLE dance

Bluetooth Low Energy is the bridge between Amiwo's hardware and the phone app. It's a protocol designed by committee, full of edge cases and gotchas.

Getting reliable connections across different phones, OS versions, and environmental conditions is harder than any backend I've built. The wireless medium is hostile. Packets disappear. Connections drop. You build resilience or fail.

making invisible forces visible

The philosophical goal of Amiwo is visibility. UV radiation is invisible. Air quality is invisible. But they affect us constantly.

The device takes these hidden forces and makes them legible. A number. A color. A notification. Suddenly you can respond to what you couldn't perceive.

There's something almost esoteric about this. Making the invisible visible. Isn't that what all measurement does? What science does?

lessons so far

  • Start with the sensor, not the form factor
  • BLE reliability > BLE features
  • Test with real users in real conditions early
  • The enclosure matters more than you think
  • Power budget is the hidden constraint

Still building. Still learning.
