tokenization beyond finance

2024.10 · 4 min

The crypto world talks about tokenization primarily as a financial mechanism. Tokenize real estate. Tokenize stocks. Tokenize art. Turn illiquid assets into tradeable tokens.

This is fine, but it's a limited view. Tokenization is more fundamentally about creating handles for things. And that's useful far beyond finance.

handles and references

A token is just a reference — a unique identifier that points to something else. File descriptors are tokens. URLs are tokens. Pointers are tokens. The token itself is cheap and movable; the thing it references may be heavy and stationary.

What blockchains add is: immutable, decentralized, globally-readable reference systems. Anyone can create a reference. Anyone can verify it. No central authority controls the namespace.

tellura and physical tokens

This is why Tellura interests me. We're not just making copper tradeable (it already is, on commodity exchanges). We're creating a handle to physical copper that can exist in software systems.

A copper warrant token can:

  • Be used as collateral in DeFi protocols
  • Be fractionalized (own 0.1% of a copper warrant)
  • Trigger automated actions when conditions are met
  • Be composed with other tokens in novel ways

None of this requires the copper to move. The token is a handle; the handle enables composition.

beyond assets

What else could benefit from decentralized, verifiable handles?

  • Identity claims — "I am over 18" without revealing who you are
  • Credentials — verifiable proof you completed something
  • Permissions — transferable access rights
  • Commitments — cryptographic promises that can be verified

The pattern is: take something that currently requires trust or a central authority, create a token, and suddenly it can participate in trustless, composable systems.

This is early. The tooling is rough. But the design space is vast.
